Philosophers

  • Marcus Aurelius
      Marcus Aurelius, Roman emperor from 161 to 180 CE, is best known for his philosophical work "Meditations," a reflection of Stoic principles. Born in 121 CE in Rome and passing away in 180 CE, he symbolizes the Golden Age of the Roman Empire. His reign faced challenges, but he is remembered for personal integrity. "Meditations" provides insights into Stoic values and has left a lasting legacy, showcasing the thoughtful mind of a Roman emperor.
